AI Cold-Calling System

Built an autonomous AI voice system that makes 100+ calls daily, qualifies leads on the phone, and automatically shuts itself down if anything goes wrong. Runs 5 days/week with zero human intervention.

Role
Built and operated end-to-end
Duration
4 weeks to production
Stack
n8n, Retell AI, Google Sheets, Slack
The Situation

Email and LinkedIn outreach take days to weeks for replies. Phone connects instantly, but nobody can make 100+ cold calls a day while running campaigns. And automated calling systems are dangerous: one bug can over-call leads, burn phone numbers, or rack up telephony costs with no one watching. The system needed to be fully autonomous and provably safe.

The System

6 interconnected workflows forming a complete AI calling operation:

  1. Outbound engine
    Runs hourly, Mon–Fri, 9AM–6PM in each lead's timezone. Pulls eligible leads (not already called, not exhausted, not terminal), initiates AI voice calls. Each lead gets up to 3 attempts with 24-hour cooldowns.
  2. Post-call qualification
    AI determines whether a real conversation happened (not voicemail). Extracts qualification data: team size, CRM used, objections, referral contacts. Updates lead status. Sends Slack alert for qualified leads.
  3. Watchdog circuit breaker
    Every 30 minutes, cross-references actual call logs against the lead database. Auto-kills the entire system and alerts the team if it detects: over-calling, terminal leads being re-called, mass failures, or data drift.
  4. Callback handler
    When a lead calls back, the system looks up their context (name, company, industry) and responds with a personalized AI agent.

n8n (6 workflows), Retell AI API, Google Sheets API, Slack webhooks. State machine with 5 statuses and 2 terminal states. 4-layer loop prevention: item-level status updates (not batch), 24-hour cooldown timestamps, permanent terminal statuses, hard cap on attempts. Circuit breaker checks 6 anomaly conditions every 30 minutes. Google Sheets chosen over a database because n8n has native integration and the sales team needed live visibility into call status without learning a new tool.
